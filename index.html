Features

Built for your everyday

Every feature in Saaci was shaped by the realities of managing sickle cell disease.

📓
Health Logging
Record symptoms, energy levels, and daily wellbeing in one place. Spot patterns over time and arrive at appointments prepared.
💧
Hydration Tracking
Set your daily water goal and log intake with a tap. Staying hydrated is one of the most important parts of sickle cell management.
🌡️
Pain Tracking
Log pain location, intensity, and duration to build a clear picture of your patterns. Your pain history is always ready when you need it.
🌙
Mood Tracking
Track how you're feeling day to day and look back over time to understand what helps and what doesn't.
💊
Medication Reminders
Never miss a dose. Set up reminders, log what you've taken, and keep a clear medication history at your fingertips.
📅
Appointment Support
Prepare for your next clinic visit with your logged health data. Know what to say, what to ask, and feel confident walking in the door.
🩸
Transfusion & Iron Labs
Log transfusion dates and track ferritin and iron lab results over time, because managing iron overload matters too.
⚠️
Flare Risk
Saaci estimates how likely you may be to have an episode based on recent patterns such as hydration, pain frequency, mood, stress, and overall symptom activity. It is an early warning support feature, not a diagnosis.
🆘
Emergency Medical Card
Store your key medical information — diagnosis, allergies, medications, and emergency contacts — so it's accessible when it matters most.
🫁
Guided Breathwork
Calming, evidence-informed breathing exercises designed to help you manage stress, anxiety, and moments of crisis.
📚
Sickle Cell Education Library
Learn about your condition in plain, accessible language — from crises and hydroxyurea to iron chelation.
🤖
AI Nurse Support
Saaci's AI Nurse provides general information and guidance to help you understand your condition clearly and calmly between appointments.

Why Saaci exists

Created by someone who cares

Saaci was created out of a genuine need for better support and organisation for people living with sickle cell disease. The reality of managing this condition — the pain, the appointments, the medications, the lab tests results, the fatigue — often comes with very little in the way of practical day-to-day tools designed with you in mind.

The founder behind Saaci, Eunice Bissaka, who independently designed and built the app from scratch, has seen and experienced how overwhelming it can be to navigate a chronic illness without the right support structure. That experience became the foundation for everything Saaci is built to do.

The goal is not to replace your care team. The goal is to help you show up to appointments informed, to understand your day-to-day patterns better, and to feel less alone while managing a serious condition.

🎯

Our mission: To give people living with sickle cell disease the tools, clarity, and calm they deserve every day.

About

A calmer way to live with sickle cell

Saaci was created to give people living with sickle cell disease the organised, supportive, and empowering tools that are so often missing from the everyday experience of managing a chronic condition.

What is Saaci?

More than a health tracker

Saaci is a mobile app built specifically for people living with sickle cell disease. It brings together health logging, hydration tracking, pain management, medication reminders, appointment preparation, breathwork, education, emergency information, and flare risk support in one calm, well-designed space.

Most health apps are generic. They are built for a general audience and ask you to fit your experience around their templates. Saaci starts from the other direction: it is built around the specific lived realities of sickle cell disease.

The features in Saaci are not there just to look impressive on a feature list. They reflect what people actually deal with: tracking ferritin levels, logging pain episodes, managing hydroxyurea, preparing for haematology appointments, staying hydrated, and finding moments of calm on difficult days.

🎯

Our mission

To give people living with sickle cell disease the tools, clarity, and calm they deserve every day.

🌍

Our vision

A world where chronic illness is managed with the same quality of support, information, and dignity as any other health condition.

Why it exists

Sickle cell is underserved. That needs to change.

People living with sickle cell disease often have to manage a huge amount of health information without having the right tools to organise it clearly. That gap is what Saaci exists to help close.

Saaci was created from a place of genuine care and understanding of how overwhelming chronic illness management can feel, especially between appointments, hospital visits, and moments of crisis.

The app is not about replacing doctors or clinical care. It is about strengthening what happens in between: the structure, awareness, and support that make day-to-day life more manageable.

Dailysupport for symptom awareness, hydration, logging, and self-management
Clearerpatterns for pain, mood, and triggers over time

Yes. We take data privacy seriously. Read our Privacy Policy for full details on how your data is collected, used, and protected.

Can I use Saaci in a medical emergency?

No. Saaci is not an emergency service. If you are experiencing a crisis, contact your care team or emergency services immediately.

What is flare risk?

Flare risk is Saaci's support feature that estimates whether your recent patterns suggest a higher chance of an episode. It may consider things like hydration, pain frequency, mood, stress, and symptom activity. It is not a diagnosis or emergency tool.
